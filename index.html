Amanda Mar is an American R&B/Pop performer and songwriter from Santa Barbara, Ca. Her debut album available now! Find it on: ITunes, CD Baby, Amazon Prime, Spotify, Napster, Apple Music etc. Videos for her first two singles can now be seen on Youtube.

 

 

 

In addition to singing with music legends Stevie Wonder, and local humanitarian Kenny Loggins, Amanda has been featured singing the Star Spangled Banner for UCSB Soccer, Mission City Roller Derby and Santa Barbara Polo & Racquet Club as well as featured in their magazine. Amanda Mar lyricist and leader of three piece band “La Radia” in 2013. La Radia performed around for a year before dispersing in early 2014.

 

 

 

Early years: Amanda has been singing and keeping rhythm since she could make sound and move. Her mom remembers her dancing to the beat of the washing machine at age 4. At the age of 6 ½ Amanda's grandmother taught her and her older sister Kristin basic harmony while she played the organ. In high school she taught herself to sing, belting out Mariah Carey and Whitney Houston to pass the time. Her mom and others encouraged her to join choir where she and her teacher found that singing soprano was her gift. This started her formal training developing her large range

 

 

 After high school she sang backup harmony and occasional lead for several bands including the Anthony Prieto Band. This was around the time Amanda met Carlos Guillen of La Cima Music Group who would go on to produce and co-write her debut album.

 

 

Amanda was chosen to join the Hollywood Pop Academy for advanced vocal training, stage performance, songwriting and hip-hop dance. Which led to auditions to The Voice, America's Got Talent, and American Idol. Amanda has an extensive musical resume, including working with the M.I.'s gospel choir alongside Troy Laureta (Ariana Grande’s turing director) and The Voice contestant, Cheesa!! .

 

 

 

Later, in 2008 and 2011, the M.I. gospel choir was invited to sing for Stevie Wonder's “House Full of Toys” concert. Amanda Mar was selected to perform a solo in both concerts with Stevie. She took up Jazz improv, beginning piano and fundamentals of music at SBCC, this is what started her journey as an independent singer, band leader and entrepreneur.

 

 

 

In 2013 she approached her friend Carlos again wanting to record a legit album with the writings as her life and experiences as the content. This is where the fun began!!!
